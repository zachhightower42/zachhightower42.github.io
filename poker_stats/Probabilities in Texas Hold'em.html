Probabilities in Texas Hold'em

Introduction

An understanding of basic probabilities will give your poker game a stronger foundation, for all game types. This article discusses all the important, and interesting, probabilities that you should be aware of.

Probabilities in poker

Probability means the degree of certainty that a possible event will occur. The classic definition is: "The probability of an event is the ratio of the number of cases favorable to it, to the number of all cases possible when nothing leads us to expect that any one of these cases should occur more than any other, which renders them, for us, equally possible." The probability of flipping heads in a coin toss is therefore 1 to 2, or 50%.

For online poker players, stochastics is the most interesting part of studying probability. Stochatics deals with frequence-based probabilities. Combinatorics (card combinations), statistics (sample size) and other subdisciplines are all a part of stochastics.

Probabilities are always a number between 0 and 1 and are usually expressed as a percentage. Probability can also be given as odds, which tells how often one event will occur in relation to the number of times another event will take place (1:2).

In the following sections you will get an overview of many useful probabilities that will help you improve your game. You can see the calculations used to arrive at these results on the second page of this article.

Hi masheepsangari (@21)

You're right, I believe it's a mistake and they used wrong formula. When calculated it does give 0.397...% that you mention.
I calculated it myself and the probability of 11.5% is correct.

We have:
C1, C2 - cards of rank C that make the pocket pair
C3, C4 - cards of the rank C left in the deck of remaining 50 cards
E - the event of hitting a set only on the flop (while holding a pocket pair)

To hit a set only on the flop it means that we have hit either C3 or C4 and the remaining 2 cards are random.

To calculate number of events corresponding to E we will start from the back end:
Let's pick those 2 cards. From 48 cards we pick 2:
48! / [2!(48-2)!]
Now, those 2 cards come with the 3rd card - C3 or C4 so that makes twice that number:
because 2! = 2
we have:
48! / (48-2)! = 48! / 46! = 48*47 = 2256
and that's the number of events corresponding to E.

Total number of events (all possible flops): 19600

Now it's easy:
2256/19600 = 0.115... = 11.5%

Hope this helps!
